Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is Earth's closest neighbour. Venus is often called Earth's twin because of the similarity in shape, size, and composition. It is the third-smallest planet in the solar system with a diameter of about 7519 km. The planet takes around 255 Earth days to orbit the Sun. A day on Venus is 243 Earth days long.

Surface Features

Venus has a hot and dry surface. Most of the planet is covered with flat and smooth plains. The planet also has level grounds, mountains, canyons, and valleys. Around 35 per cent of the surface area of the planet comprises mountainous regions. The highest feature on the planet is Maxwell. It is around 1.6 km high and about 870.65 km long.

Atmosphere

Venus has a dense atmosphere. It is 90 times heavier than the Earth's atmosphere. The thick atmosphere traps most of the Sun's heat. The planet is covered with several layers of clouds. The high density and surface temperature makes it impossible for any meteor or spacecraft to pass through the atmosphere. Strong winds of more than about 349.227 km/h blow in the upper atmosphere.

Density and Mass

The mass of Venus is about four-fifth of Earth's mass. An object that weighs 45 kg on Earth will weigh about 40kg on Venus. Venus is also slightly less dense than Earth.
